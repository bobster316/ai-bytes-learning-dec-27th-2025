Most AI strategy documents are works of corporate fiction. They describe what AI could theoretically accomplish rather than what the organisation is actually equipped to deliver in the next 12 months. This gap has a name: the Vision-Value gap — and crossing it requires a framework, not a vision statement.

"The organisations that succeed don't have the best data. They have a repeatable process for choosing which problem to solve first."

The 4-Pillar Framework emerged from analysing 340 AI transformations across 2019–2024. The key insight wasn't about technology — it was about sequence. Order of operations determines outcomes.

◈
Key Takeaways 3 points
1
AI strategy is a selection problem before a technology problem. Choose use cases by business impact, not technical novelty.
2
The 4 pillars must be implemented in sequence. You cannot scale what you haven't governed, and you cannot govern what you haven't selected.
3
A 90-day roadmap beats a 3-year strategy every time. Specificity forces prioritisation. Early wins build the trust needed for the next phase.
