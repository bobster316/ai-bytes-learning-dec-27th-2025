# ✍️ AI Bytes Content Style Guide: The Human Standard

This document defines the voice of AI Bytes. We are a platform built by humans, for humans. We reject "AI marketing fluff" in favor of clear, tangible, and grounded language.

## 🚫 The "No AI-isms" Rule
We do not use abstract, robotic, or "hype" words that are commonly generated by LLMs. If a sentence sounds like it was written by ChatGPT, **rewrite it**.

### ❌ Banned Words (The "AI-List")
| Banned Term ("AI-ism") | Why it's banned | ✅ Use these instead |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mastery** | Abstract, pretentious, overused. | **Skills, Capability, Expertise, Knowledge** |
| **Unleash** | Hyperbolic, cliché. | **Build, Use, Start, Create** |
| **Elevate** | Corporate jargon. | **Improve, Upgrade, Boost, Fix** |
| **Dive Deep** | Overused metaphor. | **Explore, Examine, Study, Look at** |
| **Landscape** | Vague (e.g., "AI Landscape"). | **Market, Industry, World, Field** |
| **Realm** | Fantasy language. | **Area, Topic, Field** |
| **Tapestry** | AI poetics (never use this). | **Mix, System, Collection** |
| **Orchestrate** | Too complex/robotic. | **Manage, Control, Organize** |
| **Symphony** | Flowery metaphor. | **Combination, System** |
| **Empower** | Passive corporate speak. | **Give you tools, Help you, Enable** |
| **Unlock** | (Unless literal unlocking) Overused. | **Get, Access, Use** |

## 🗣 Our Voice: "The Pragmatic Engineer"
We sound like a senior engineer explaining something to a smart junior developer.

- **We are:** Direct, Specific, Honest, Tactic.
- **We are NOT:** Poetic, Flowery, "Sales-y", Vague.

### Examples
❌ **Bad (AI Style):**
"Embark on a journey to master the realm of Generative AI and unleash your potential to orchestrate digital symphonies."

✅ **Good (Human Style):**
"Learn how to use Generative AI to built real tools. We'll show you how to manage complex workflows and create better software."

❌ **Bad (AI Style):**
"Elevate your understanding of the prompt engineering landscape."

✅ **Good (Human Style):**
"Get better at writing prompts that actually work."

## 📝 The "Real-World" Test
Before publishing any text, ask:
1.  **"Can I touch it?"** (Is it a real outcome like a certificate or a tool, or a vague concept like "potential"?)
2.  **"Would a human say this at a pub?"** (If you wouldn't say "I'm mastering the realm of data" to a friend, don't write it.)
3.  **"Is it specific?"** (Don't say "valuable skills", say "Python automation".)
