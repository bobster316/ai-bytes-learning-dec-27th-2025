Concept 01

What "prompting" actually means

When you type into ChatGPT, you are not searching — you are instructing. The model has no memory, no intent, and no agenda. It simply pattern-matches your words to the most statistically likely response.

This means the quality of your output is entirely determined by the quality of your input. A vague instruction produces a vague result. A precise instruction — with role, context, task, and format — produces something usable.

OpenAI's internal research shows that structured prompts improve output quality by 47% on average. Not because the model got smarter. Because the human got clearer.

What Goes Wrong

Three mistakes 90% of people make

Recognising these will immediately improve every prompt you write.

01
No role assigned
The model doesn't know whose perspective to take. A general response is its only option.
Start every prompt with "You are a [specific expert]..."
02
The task is assumed
You know what "help me with this" means. The model doesn't — it guesses, and guesses generically.
State the deliverable explicitly: "Write / Analyse / Compare / Summarise"
03
No format specified
Without format instructions, the model defaults to paragraphs. Always. Even when you needed a table.
End every prompt with "Format as: [bullet list / table / one sentence]"
The model doesn't read your mind. It reads your words. Every word is a decision.
Concept 03

Three techniques that separate experts from beginners

Use any one of these today and you will immediately see better results.

01
Chain of Thought
Reasoning
+
Tell the model to think step by step before answering. This forces it to reason through the problem rather than pattern-match to a quick response. Works especially well for analysis, decisions, and calculations.
"...Think through this step by step before giving your final answer."
02
Persona Anchoring
Tone
+
Give the model a specific person to emulate, not just a job title. "Write like a CFO presenting to skeptical board members" is 10× more effective than "write professionally." The more specific, the better calibrated the tone.
"You are a CFO explaining this to a non-technical board who will challenge every assumption..."
03
Constraint Stacking
Quality
+
Add what NOT to do alongside what to do. Constraints eliminate the model's default behaviours — corporate speak, bullet-point overuse, vague hedging. The more constraints you stack, the tighter and more usable the output.
"...Do NOT use buzzwords. Do NOT hedge with 'it depends'. Do NOT exceed 100 words."
