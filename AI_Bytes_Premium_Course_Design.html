After this lesson: you can score any AI use case on a 2×2 Impact vs Feasibility matrix in under 10 minutes.

Most AI strategy documents are works of corporate fiction. They describe what AI could do in theory, not what the organisation is actually equipped to deliver in the next 12 months. The gap between aspiration and execution has a name: the Vision-Value gap — and crossing it requires a framework, not a vision statement.

The 4-Pillar Framework was built from analysing 340 AI transformations across financial services, manufacturing, and professional services between 2019 and 2024. The organisations that succeeded didn't necessarily have the best data or the most sophisticated models. They had a repeatable process for choosing which problem to solve first.

⬡ Key Takeaways
AI strategy is a selection problem before it is a technology problem — choose use cases by business impact, not technical novelty.
The 4 pillars must be implemented in sequence: you cannot scale what you haven't governed, and you cannot govern what you haven't selected.
A 90-day roadmap beats a 3-year strategy every time — it forces specificity and creates visible wins that build organisational trust.
