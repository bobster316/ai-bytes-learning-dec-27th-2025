# 🎓 Welcome to AI Bytes Learning: The Newbie Guide

Hello! If you're new here, this guide will explain exactly what this platform is, how it works, and what "magic" is happening under the hood—without all the scary tech-speak.

---

## 🌟 1. The Big Idea: "15-Minute Breakthroughs"
Most online learning is boring and way too long. We do things differently. 
We break huge, complex AI topics into **"Bytes"**—15-minute lessons that give you a "breakthrough" moment. By the time you finish a "Byte," you should be able to actually *do* something new.

### Our Secret Recipe (The DNA):
- **Metaphors First**: We explain AI using things you already know (like comparing a Neural Network to a "Decision-making Committee").
- **The Rule of 4**: Every lesson has exactly 4 visuals. No more, no less. It keeps your brain focused.
- **Zero Fluff**: We value your time. If it's not important, it's not in the lesson.

---

## 🚀 2. How it Works (The Student Journey)
1. **The Catalog**: You pick a course (like "AI Foundations").
2. **The Dashboard**: You track your "Velocity" (how fast you're learning) and your **Momentum** (your consistency over time).
3. **The Lesson**: You read a Byte, look at the 4 visuals, and then do a 5-minute practical task.
4. **Sterling**: Our AI Professor, Sterling, is always in the corner. You can talk to him, and he’ll explain things in his sophisticated (and slightly sarcastic) British accent.

---

## ⚡ 3. Decoding "Momentum"
We don't use the word "streaks" because it sounds like a game. We use **Momentum** because learning AI is like moving a heavy engine:

- **The Push**: On Day 1, it takes effort to start.
- **The Flow**: By Day 3, the engine is humming. It's easier to keep going than it is to stop and start over.
- **Peak Velocity**: When you maintain a "Momentum" of 7+ days, your brain stops fighting the new concepts and starts absorbing them automatically.

**How to maintain Momentum:** Just finish one 15-minute "Byte" every 24 hours. Sterling will track this for you on your dashboard.

## 🛠 4. What's "Under the Hood"? (The Tech Stack)
You don't need to be a coder to understand this, but here is what makes the site run:

- **The Engine (Next.js)**: Think of this as the chassis of a car. It holds everything together and making sure the pages load lightning-fast.
- **The Brains (Gemini & Groq)**: These are the AI models. 
    - **Gemini** handles Sterling's voice and real-time talking.
    - **Groq** helps us generate the course content in seconds.
- **The Memory (Supabase)**: This is our database. It remembers who you are, what lessons you've finished, and your quiz scores.
- **The Payments (Stripe)**: This handles the "Byte Pass" subscriptions securely.
