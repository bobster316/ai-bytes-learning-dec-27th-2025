Concept 01 · Step 2 of 4
The Role tells the model whose mind to borrow.
Without a role, the model defaults to an average of every voice it has ever read — nobody in particular. Five words change everything. "You are a senior UX researcher" focuses knowledge, vocabulary, and perspective immediately.
Concept 01 · Step 3 of 4
Context turns a general tool into a specific expert.
Telling the model your situation is like handing a contractor the blueprints before work begins. Without it, they build something plausible but generic. With it, every word is calibrated to your specific reality.
Concept 01 · Step 4 of 4
Task and Format eliminate the guesswork.
The task is what you want produced. The format is how you want it delivered. Without both, the model guesses conservatively — defaulting to paragraphs, generalities, and hedging. With both, it knows exactly what success looks like.

Concept 03

Three techniques that separate
experts from everyone else

01
Chain of Thought
Reasoning
Tell the model to think step by step before answering. Forces reasoning over pattern-matching. Works especially well for analysis, decisions, and multi-step calculations.
"…Think through this step by step before giving your final answer."
02
Persona Anchoring
Tone
Give the model a specific person to emulate, not just a job title. "Write like a CFO presenting to a sceptical board" is 10× more effective than "write professionally."
"You are a CFO explaining this to a non-technical board who will challenge every assumption…"
03
Constraint Stacking
Quality
Add what NOT to do alongside what to do. Constraints eliminate the model's default behaviours — corporate speak, vague hedging, bullet overuse. More constraints, tighter output.
"…Do NOT use buzzwords. Do NOT hedge. Do NOT exceed 100 words."
